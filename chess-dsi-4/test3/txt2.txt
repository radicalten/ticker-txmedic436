p1: I have a C file of a chess game engine for DSi that never finishes writing the engine text on the bottom screen, can you help me troubleshoot that:

A: The issue you are experiencing—where the engine text on the bottom screen "never finishes writing" or appears truncated/cut off—is caused by Console Auto-Scrolling and Text Wrapping on the Nintendo DS/DSi hardware.
Why this happens:

    The 32-Column Screen Wrap Limit: The DS sub-screen is exactly 32 columns wide and 24 rows high. If any printed line exceeds 32 characters, the console library automatically wraps the text to the next line.
    Flexible Width Format Bug: Format strings like %-5s specify a minimum width, not a maximum. A complex SAN move (like exd8=Q+ or Q1xd8=Q#) can easily be 7 or 8 characters long. Since %-5s does not truncate, the column widths expand, pushing the total line length beyond 32 characters.
    Cumulative Vertical Scrolling: Once lines wrap, they occupy extra rows. This pushes the lower text elements past Row 24. On the DSi, writing past Row 24 triggers a hardware viewport scroll. Once scrolled, your absolute resetting command (\x1b[1;1H) starts writing from the new scrolled "top" rather than the physical top of the screen, permanently breaking your layout and causing the bottom text to slide off-screen.
    Engine Info Metrics Overflow: During active thinking, fast engines output high NPS and Depth figures. This can easily cause the CPU evaluation and Speed lines to exceed 32 characters and trigger a screen-breaking scroll.

How this code fixes it:

    Absolute Row Positioning (\x1b[row;1H): Instead of relying on relative \n line-breaks (which shift dynamically when text wraps), every line is written with absolute cursor positioning. Even if a line accidentally wraps, the next line snaps back to its correct row instead of scrolling the interface.
    Strict Move Column Truncation (%-5.5s): The SAN move columns now utilize a maximum truncation rule. Long move codes are strictly limited to 5 characters, guaranteeing your column alignment never wraps.
    Tighter metric layout: Compresses the engine evaluation/thinking line so that it never exceeds 28 characters under any processing conditions.
    Safe Log buffer truncation: Restricts raw engine console entries to 28 characters to leave a safe margin of error on the display boundary.
